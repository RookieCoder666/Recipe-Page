    Whole Wheat Pancakes with Fruit Compote

    These fluffy and delicious whole wheat pancakes are a healthy and satisfying vegetarian breakfast option. Packed with fiber from the whole wheat flour and topped with a sweet and vibrant fruit compote, they're a great way to start your day.

    Recipe Information

    Prep Time : 10 minutes

    Cook Time : 5-7 minutes per batch of pancakes

    Total Time : 15-17 minutes

    Servings : 2-3 pancakes

    Nutritional Information (per serving, about 2 pancakes) :

    • Calories : Around 200-250 (depending on the size of your pancakes)
    • Protein : Around 5g from the plant-based milk and flour
    • Carbs : Around 30-40g from the flour and milk
    • Fat : Around 5g from the oil

    Ingredients :

    • 1 ½ cups whole wheat flour
    • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
    • ¼ teaspoon baking soda
    • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • Pinch of salt
    • 1 ¼ cups unsweetened plant-based milk (almond milk, soy milk, or oat milk all work well)
    • 1 tablespoon melted coconut oil (or vegetable oil)
    • 1 tablespoon flaxseed meal mixed with 3 tablespoons water

    Cooking Tips and Substitutions :

    • For extra fluffy pancakes, separate the flaxseed egg mixture and whisk the egg whites until stiff peaks form. Then, gently fold the egg whites into the batter.
    • Don't have coconut oil? You can use vegetable oil or melted vegan butter instead.
    • Want to add some extra flavor? Add a teaspoon of vanilla extract to the batter.
    • For a fun twist, add a handful of chopped nuts or chocolate chips (dairy-free) to the batter.

    Instructions :

    1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
    2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the plant-based milk, melted coconut oil, and whisked flaxseed meal and water.
    3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and whisk until just combined. Be careful not to overmix! A few lumps are okay.
    4. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or skillet over medium heat.
    5. Pour ¼ cup batter per pancake onto the griddle.
    6. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through. You can peek underneath to check if the edges are starting to set.
    7. Serve immediately with your favorite toppings like fruit compote and a drizzle of maple syrup.

    For the Fruit Compote :

    Nutritional Information (per serving) (This will vary depending on the fruit used) :

    • Calories : Around 50-100 (depending on the fruit)
    • Carbs : Around 10-20g from the fruit
    • Fat : Around 0-5g from the fruit (depending on the fruit)

    Ingredients :

    • 1 cup fresh or frozen mixed berries (blueberries, raspberries, strawberries)
    • ¼ cup water
    • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (or agave nectar)
    • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, for thickening)

    Cooking Tips and Substitutions :

    • Feel free to experiment with different fruits! Sliced peaches, apples, or chopped mango would all be delicious additions.
    • For a thicker compote, whisk the cornstarch with a tablespoon of water before adding it to the saucepan with the berries.
    • Like your compote a bit sweeter? Add an extra tablespoon of maple syrup or agave nectar.

    Instructions :

    1. In a saucepan, combine the berries, water, and maple syrup.
    2. Bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the fruit softens and the mixture thickens slightly. If using cornstarch, add it during the last minute of cooking and whisk continuously until thickened.
    3. Remove from the heat and let cool slightly before serving.

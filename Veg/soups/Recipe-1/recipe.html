    Quesadillas

    These protein-packed and flavorful vegetarian breakfast quesadillas are a perfect way to start your day. They're customizable with your favorite fillings, easy to assemble in under 15 minutes, and entirely vegetarian!

    Recipe Information

    Prep Time : 5 minutes

    Cook Time : 5-7 minutes per quesadilla

    Total Time : 10-12 minutes

    Servings : 2 quesadillas

    Nutritional Information (per serving) :

    • Calories : Around 300-400 (depending on the fillings and cheese)
    • Protein : Around 10-15g from the beans and cheese (if using
    • Carbs : Around 30-40g from the tortillas and vegetables
    • Fat : Around 10-15g from the cheese and oil

    Ingredients :

    • 2 large flour tortillas (burrito-size)
    • 1 cup cooked black beans (or pinto beans)
    • ½ cup crumbled queso fresco cheese (or another vegetarian cheese like shredded mozzarella, or omit for a vegan option)
    • ½ cup chopped mushrooms
    • ½ cup chopped bell peppers
    • ¼ cup chopped onion
    • ½ cup chopped tomatoes (roma or cherry tomatoes work well)
    • ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Olive oil
    • Optional additions: Sliced avocado, salsa, dollop of Greek yogurt

    Cooking Tips and Substitutions :

    • Feel free to experiment with different vegetables! Chopped zucchini, broccoli florets, or spinach would all be delicious additions.
    • Don't have fresh vegetables? You can use a pre-chopped frozen vegetable mix to save time.
    • Short on time? Pre-cook your black beans the night before.
    • For a spicier option, add a diced jalapeno pepper to the vegetables when cooking.
    • Make it vegan? Simply omit the cheese and use a plant-based yogurt instead of Greek yogurt (if using).
    • Serve your quesadilla with a side of fruit salad or chopped vegetables for a more complete breakfast.

    Instructions :

    1. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Add a drizzle of olive oil.
    2. Sauté the chopped vegetables (mushrooms, bell peppers, onion, tomatoes) for 5-7 minutes, or until softened. Season with salt and pepper.
    3. Warm the tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave for a few seconds to make them pliable.
    4. Lay a warmed tortilla flat on a plate. Spread a layer of cooked black beans down the center.
    5. Top the beans with your cooked vegetables and cheese (if using).
    6. Sprinkle with chopped cilantro.
    7. Fold the bottom flap of the tortilla over the filling, then fold in the sides. Carefully roll the tortilla up tightly.
    8. Repeat with the remaining tortilla and fillings.
    9. Heat a lightly oiled skillet or griddle over medium heat.
    10. Place the quesadilla on the hot skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy.
    11. Cut in half and serve immediately with your favorite toppings like salsa, sliced avocado, or a dollop of Greek yogurt (if using).

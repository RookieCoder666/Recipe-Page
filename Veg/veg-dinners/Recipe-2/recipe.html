    Vegetarian Chili with Cornbread

    Vegetarian Chili with Cornbread is a perfect weeknight meal. It's hearty and flavorful, packed with protein from the beans, and the cornbread adds a delicious warm and bready element. Plus, both the chili and cornbread are relatively easy to make.

    Recipe Information

    Prep Time : 10 minutes

    Cook Time : 30 minutes

    Total Time : 40 minutes

    Servings : 4-6


    Ingredients :

    For the Chili:

    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 1 medium onion, chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
    • 1 (15 oz) can diced tomatoes, undrained
    • 1 (15 oz) can crushed tomatoes, undrained
    • 1 (15 oz) can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
    • 1 (15 oz) can black beans, drained and rinsed
    • 1 (15 oz) can pinto beans, drained and rinsed (optional)
    • 1 cup vegetable broth
    • 2 cups water
    • 1 tablespoon chili powder
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
    • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Toppings (optional): Chopped fresh cilantro, shredded cheese, sour cream, avocado slices

    For the Cornbread (Optional):

    • 1 box cornbread mix (or follow your favorite cornbread recipe)

    Cooking Tips and Substitutions :

    • Feel free to experiment with different vegetables! Chopped zucchini, corn kernels, or chopped mushrooms would all be delicious additions.
    • Don't have fresh vegetables? You can use a pre-chopped frozen vegetable mix to save time.
    • Like it spicier? Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or chopped jalapeno pepper while sauteing the vegetables.
    • For a richer flavor, add a tablespoon of tomato paste along with the canned tomatoes.
    • This chili is even more flavorful the next day, so feel free to make a big batch for meal prep!

    Instructions :


    Make the Chili:

    1. Heat Oil and Saut√© Vegetables: Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 30 seconds, until fragrant. Stir in the chopped green bell pepper and cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until softened.
    2. Add Canned Tomatoes and Broth: Pour in the diced tomatoes (undrained) and crushed tomatoes (undrained). Add the vegetable broth and water. Stir to combine.
    3. Incorporate Beans and Spices: Add the drained and rinsed kidney beans, black beans, and pinto beans (if using). Season with chili powder, ground cumin, dried oregano, smoked paprika (optional), salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
    4. Simmer and Thicken: Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the flavors have melded and the chili has thickened slightly. Taste and adjust seasonings with additional salt and pepper, if desired.

    Prepare the Cornbread (Optional):

    1. Preheat your oven according to the package instructions for your cornbread mix. Prepare the cornbread batter following the package directions.
    2. Pour the cornbread batter into a greased baking pan. Bake for the recommended time, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

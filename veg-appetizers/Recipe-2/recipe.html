    Vegan Potato & Cheese Samosas

    These crispy and flavorful samosas are a delicious vegetarian appetizer or snack, perfect for satisfying your cravings. This recipe uses vegan cheese to keep it entirely plant-based, while still delivering that cheesy goodness!

    Recipe Information

    Prep Time : 20 minutes

    Cook Time : 15-20 minutes per batch

    Total Time : 35-40 minutes

    Servings : 4-6 samosas

    Nutritional Information (per serving, about 2 samosas) :

    • Calories : Around 250-300 (depending on the oil used)
    • Protein : Around 5-7g from the vegan cheese and flour
    • Carbs : Around 30-40g from the flour and potatoes
    • Fat : Around 5-10g from the oil

    Ingredients :

    For the Filling :

    • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
    • ½ cup chopped onion
    • 1 green chili pepper, chopped (optional, for spice)
    • 1 clove garlic, minced
    • ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
    • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
    • ½ teaspoon ground coriander
    • ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder
    • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 cup vegan cheese, shredded (choose a melty variety)

    For the Dough :

    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (plus extra for frying)
    • water

    Cooking Tips and Substitutions :

    • Feel free to experiment with different vegetables! Chopped peas, carrots, or bell peppers would be delicious additions to the filling.
    • Don't have fresh vegetables? You can use a pre-chopped frozen vegetable mix to save time.
    • Like a bit more moisture? Add a tablespoon of chopped fresh mint to the filling.
    • For a gluten-free option, use a gluten-free flour blend that works well for pastries.
    • Short on time? You can use store-bought vegan puff pastry dough instead of making your own dough. Thaw according to package instructions.

    Instructions :


    For the Filling :

    1. In a large bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, chopped onion, green chili pepper (if using), garlic, chopped cilantro, vegetable oil, ground cumin, ground coriander, turmeric powder, red pepper flakes (if using), salt, and pepper to taste.
    2. Add the shredded vegan cheese and mix well to combine.

    For the Dough

    1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt.
    2. Add the vegetable oil and mix it in with your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    3. Slowly add water, a tablespoon at a time, until a soft dough forms. You may not need all the water. The dough should be slightly pliable but not sticky.
    4. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for a few minutes until smooth.
    5. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15 minutes.

    Assembling and Cooking the Samosas:

    1. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough into a thin sheet. Aim for about 1/8 inch thickness.
    2. Using a sharp knife or a cookie cutter, cut the dough into squares.
    3. Place a spoonful of the potato and cheese filling in the center of each dough square.
    4. Brush the edges of the dough square with a little water to help seal.
    5. Fold the dough square into a triangle shape, pressing the edges together to seal. You can use a fork to crimp the edges for an extra secure seal.
    6. Heat enough vegetable oil in a large skillet or pan over medium heat. The oil should be about ½ inch deep.
    7. Once the oil is hot, carefully place a few samosas in the pan at a time. Do not overcrowd the pan.
    8. Fry the samosas for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy.
    9. Drain the cooked samosas on paper towels to remove excess oil.
    10. Repeat steps 6-9 with the remaining samosas.

    Hot Chocolate

    Hot chocolate is a classic wintertime drink, but it's also perfect for enjoying on a chilly day any time of year.

    Recipe Information

    Ingredients :


    For the Chocolate Mixture:

    • 1 cup heavy cream
    • 8 ounces semisweet chocolate, chopped
    • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
    • Pinch of salt

    For the Milk Mixture:

    • 2 cups milk (whole milk or your preferred milk alternative)
    • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • Optional: Whipped cream and marshmallows for garnish

    Cooking Tips and Substitutions :

    • For a richer flavor, use dark chocolate instead of semisweet chocolate.
    • Make it vegan! Use vegan milk and dark chocolate for a dairy-free version.
    • Like it spicy? Add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a canela stick (Mexican cinnamon) to the milk mixture while heating.
    • For a minty twist, add a few drops of peppermint extract to the hot chocolate.
    • Get creative with toppings! Try drizzled caramel sauce, crushed nuts, or even a sprinkle of cinnamon sugar.

    Instructions :

    1. Make the Chocolate Mixture: In a medium saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until simmering. Remove from heat.
    2. Add Chocolate and Cocoa Powder: Add the chopped semisweet chocolate, unsweetened cocoa powder, and salt to the hot cream. Let it sit for 5 minutes, allowing the chocolate to soften.
    3. Whisk Until Smooth: Whisk the mixture until smooth and glossy. If the chocolate isn't fully melted, you can return the pan to low heat for a short time, whisking constantly, until melted.
    4. Make the Milk Mixture: In a separate saucepan, heat the milk and vanilla extract over medium heat until steaming (not boiling).
    5. Combine and Serve: Slowly whisk the hot milk mixture into the chocolate mixture. Taste and adjust sweetness as desired. You can add a little sugar or honey if needed.
    6. Pour and Garnish: Pour the hot chocolate into mugs and garnish with whipped cream and marshmallows (optional). Enjoy your delicious homemade hot chocolate!

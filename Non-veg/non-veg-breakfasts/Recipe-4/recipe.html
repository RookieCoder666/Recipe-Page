    Ratatouille

    This classic French dish is a colorful and flavorful medley of summer vegetables, traditionally baked in the oven. This pure veg version omits cheese and uses vegetable broth for added moisture, making it a delicious and healthy vegetarian option.

    Recipe Information

    Prep Time : 15 minutes

    Cook Time : 45-50 minutes

    Total Time : 1 hour

    Servings : 4-6

    Nutritional Information (per serving) :

    • Calories : Around 200-250 (depending on the oil used)
    • Carbs : Around 30-40g from the vegetables
    • Fat : Around 5-10g from the olive oil

    Ingredients :

    • 1 large eggplant, sliced into ½ inch rounds
    • 1 medium zucchini, sliced into ½ inch rounds
    • 1 yellow squash, sliced into ½ inch rounds
    • 1 red bell pepper, sliced into strips
    • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced into strips
    • 1 medium onion, sliced
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes, undrained
    • 1 cup vegetable broth
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
    • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Fresh chopped parsley (optional, for garnish)

    Cooking Tips and Substitutions :

    • To prevent the eggplant from browning too quickly, sprinkle the slices with salt and let them sit for 10 minutes before rinsing and patting them dry.
    • Feel free to experiment with different vegetables! Chopped broccoli florets, cherry tomatoes, or green beans would all be delicious additions.
    • Don't have fresh vegetables? You can use a pre-chopped frozen vegetable mix to save time.
    • For a richer flavor, use a tablespoon of tomato paste instead of some of the diced tomatoes.For a richer flavor, use a tablespoon of tomato paste instead of some of the diced tomatoes.
    • Like a little heat? Add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the vegetables as they cook.
    • Serve your ratatouille over a bed of rice, quinoa, or couscous for a more complete meal. You can also enjoy it as an appetizer or side dish.

    Instructions :

    1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Lightly grease a baking dish.
    2. In a large bowl, toss the eggplant slices with 1 tablespoon of olive oil and a pinch of salt. Let them sit for 10 minutes.
    3. Heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onions and cook for 5 minutes, or until softened.
    4. Add the bell peppers and cook for an additional 3-5 minutes, or until softened.
    5. Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds, until fragrant.
    6. Stir in the diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, dried thyme, and dried oregano. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes.
    7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
    8. Arrange the eggplant slices, zucchini slices, and yellow squash slices in a single layer in the prepared baking dish.
    9. Pour the tomato sauce mixture over the top of the vegetables.
    10. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes.
    11. Uncover the baking dish and continue baking for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the sauce has thickened slightly.
    12. Remove from the oven and garnish with chopped fresh parsley (optional).
    13. Serve immediately and enjoy your delicious Pure Veg Ratatouille!

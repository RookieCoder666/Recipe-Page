    Vegetable Biryani with Raita

    This fragrant and flavorful biryani features an assortment of colorful vegetables layered with basmati rice and aromatic spices. It's a delicious and satisfying vegetarian option perfect for a special occasion or a comforting weeknight meal.

    Recipe Information

    Prep Time : 15 minutes

    Cook Time : 30-35 minutes

    Total Time : 45-50 minutes

    Servings : 4-6

    Ingredients :


    For the Biryani:

    • 1 ½ cups basmati rice, rinsed and soaked for 30 minutes
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 2 cloves
    • 2 green cardamoms
    • 1 black cardamom
    • 1 small cinnamon stick
    • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
    • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
    • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
    • ½ teaspoon coriander powder
    • ¼ teaspoon garam masala powder
    • Salt to taste
    • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, green beans, cauliflower florets - chopped)
    • ½ cup chopped cashews and raisins (optional)
    • 2-3 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro (optional)
    • 2 cups water (or vegetable broth for extra flavor)

    For the Raita:

    • 1 cup whole milk yogurt (or vegan yogurt for a vegan option)
    • ½ cucumber, grated
    • ¼ cup chopped fresh mint leaves
    • 1 green chili, finely chopped (optional)
    • Salt to taste

    Cooking Tips and Substitutions :

    • Feel free to use your favorite combination of vegetables. Chopped bell peppers, corn kernels, or green beans would all be delicious additions.
    • Don't have fresh vegetables? You can use a pre-chopped frozen vegetable mix to save time.
    • For a richer flavor, add a tablespoon of ghee or melted butter along with the vegetable oil when sauteing the onions.
    • Make it vegan! Use vegan yogurt for the raita and omit the cashews if desired.
    • Adjust the spice level to your preference.

    Instructions :


    Make the Biryani:

    1. Prep the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rinsed rice in water for at least 30 minutes, or up to an hour.
    2. Heat Oil and Add Spices: Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the bay leaf, cloves, green cardamoms, black cardamom, and cinnamon stick. Let them splutter for a few seconds to release their fragrance.
    3. Sauté Onion and Ginger-Garlic Paste: Add the thinly sliced onion and cook until softened and translucent, about 5-7 minutes. Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly.
    4. Add Spices and Vegetables: Stir in the turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala powder. Cook for another 30 seconds, until fragrant. Add the chopped mixed vegetables and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until slightly softened.
    5. Layer the Biryani: Drain the soaked basmati rice and spread half of it evenly over the masala mixture in the pot. Sprinkle half of the chopped cashews and raisins (if using) over the rice. Season with salt.
    6. Add Water/Broth and Remaining Ingredients: Gently pour the water (or vegetable broth) over the rice layer. Add the remaining rice, spreading it evenly. Top with the remaining cashews and raisins (if using), and chopped fresh cilantro (if using).
    7. Steam and Cook: Cover the pot tightly with a lid and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through and the liquid is absorbed.
    8. Rest and Fluff: Once cooked, turn off the heat and let the biryani steam for an additional 5-10 minutes with the lid on. This allows the flavors to further develop. Fluff the biryani gently with a fork before serving.

    Make the Raita:

    1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the yogurt, grated cucumber, chopped mint leaves, and finely chopped green chili (if using). Season with salt to taste.

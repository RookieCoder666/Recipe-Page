    Minestrone Soup

    This classic Italian soup is traditionally made with vegetables, beans, and sometimes pasta. This pure veg version omits meat and uses vegetable broth for a flavorful and satisfying vegetarian option.

    Recipe Information

    Prep Time : 15 minutes

    Cook Time : 30-35 minutes

    Total Time : 45-50 minutes

    Servings : 4-6


    Ingredients :

    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 1 medium onion, chopped
    • 2 carrots, chopped
    • 2 celery stalks, chopped
    • 1 clove garlic, minced
    • 1 zucchini, diced
    • 1 cup chopped green beans
    • 4 cups vegetable broth
    • 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes, undrained
    • 1 (15 oz) can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
    • 1 (15 oz) can white beans (cannellini beans or Great Northern beans), drained and rinsed
    • ½ cup small pasta (optional, such as elbow macaroni or small shells)
    • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
    • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Fresh chopped parsley (optional, for garnish)

    Cooking Tips and Substitutions :

    • Feel free to experiment with different vegetables! Chopped broccoli florets, peas, or cherry tomatoes would all be delicious additions.
    • Don't have fresh vegetables? You can use a pre-chopped frozen vegetable mix to save time.
    • For a richer flavor, use a tablespoon of tomato paste instead of some of the diced tomatoes.
    • Like a little heat? Add a pinch of red pepper flakes when cooking the vegetables.
    • This soup is even more flavorful the next day, so feel free to make a big batch for meal prep!

    Instructions :

    1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
    2. Add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Cook for 5-7 minutes, or until softened.
    3. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 30 seconds, until fragrant.
    4. Add the diced zucchini and green beans. Cook for an additional 3-5 minutes, or until slightly softened.
    5. Pour in the vegetable broth and diced tomatoes (with their juices).
    6. Add the drained and rinsed kidney beans and white beans.
    7. Stir in the dried oregano and dried thyme.
    8. Bring the soup to a simmer.
    9. If using pasta, add it to the soup and cook according to package instructions, or until al dente (cooked but still firm to the bite).
    10. Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste.
    11. Reduce heat and simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the flavors have melded.
